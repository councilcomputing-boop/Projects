A Lesson From The Count

Sit. Listen well. In three centuries at the tables of Europe, I have never once lost the thread of a shoe. Tonight I teach you the method that keeps me... comfortable: the Hi-Lo count.

  1. Every card dealt — yours, the dealer's, or any other soul at the table — feeds the Running Count.
  2. When it climbs positive, the deck grows heavy with high cards and aces. The house trembles. Wager boldly.
  3. When it falls negative, only the low cards remain, and fortune favors the house. Wager little, or not at all.
  4. But the Running Count alone is a crude instrument — meaningless without context. Divide it by the decks remaining, and you have the True Count, the number I actually act upon. Visit the Deck Math tab to master this division.
  5. In Manual mode, keyboard shortcuts work too: ↑ = +1, ↓ = -1, → = 0.

Why True Count?
The Running Count alone doesn't tell you much once more than one deck is in play. A running count of +6 means the deck is loaded with high cards if there's only 1 deck left — but it's barely a blip if there are 6 decks left. To compare fairly (and know how much to bet), divide the running count by how many decks remain:
Running Count ÷ Decks Remaining = True Count
